World Air Quality Index (AQI)

An air quality index (AQI) is an indicator to communicate
to the public how polluted the air currently is or how polluted
it is forecast to become. As air pollution levels rise, so does the AQI,
along with the associated public health risk.

Quick definition:
AQI: Air quality index
PM2.5: Tiny particles or droplets in the air that are 2.5 micrometers or less in width
Ozone: Gas that form in the atmosphere through a chemical reactions
Carbon Monoxide (CO): colorless, odorless gas produced by the incomplete burning of fossil fuels
